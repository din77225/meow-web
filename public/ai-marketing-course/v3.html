Frequently Asked Questions

Is $4,150 worth it?

Consider what you're spending now. If you're paying an agency $3,000-$5,000/month for creative, this course pays for itself the first month you bring that work in-house. If you're doing everything manually, think about the hours your team spends on content creation. Even saving 10 hours/week at $50/hour, that's $2,000/month in recovered productivity. Plus, BC employers may qualify for up to 80% reimbursement through the ETG.

I'm not technical. Can my team keep up?

Absolutely. This course requires zero coding or design background. If your team can use email and social media, they can do this. Module 0 starts with a guided setup session where we walk through every tool together. The Thursday implementation labs exist specifically for hands-on help when someone gets stuck.

What if someone misses a session?

All sessions are recorded. If a team member misses a class, they can watch the recording before the next session. You'll have access to all recordings for 12 months after the course ends.

Can our employer pay for this?

Yes, and they probably should. Many employers cover professional development costs. BC-based employers may also qualify for the Employer Training Grant, which can reimburse up to 80% of eligible training costs. We provide completion certificates and hours documentation.

What tools will we need?

You'll need subscriptions to Claude Pro (~$20 USD/month) and Canva Pro (~$15 CAD/month). For AI image and video generation, we use OpenArt, which starts at $14 USD/month and includes access to multiple AI models. NotebookLM is free. Total additional cost: roughly $50-$70/month during the 6 weeks.
